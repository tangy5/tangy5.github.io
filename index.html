I am currently a fourth year doctoral student at Department of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering of Vanderbilt University. My supervisor is Professor Bennett A. Landman. Our Medical-image Analysis and Statistical Interpretation lab (MASI Lab) performs translational research to explore innovative, clinically useful techniques. I work on a wide range of challenges in statistical machine learning and translational research (data-driven deep learning) and am especially interested their application of medical image analysis, computational biomedicine, distributed computing and general healthcare. I spent great time at Nvidia Research (Santa Clara, California (remote)) and Siemens Healthineers (Princeton, New Jersey). I received a M.S. from New York University in 2018 Before this, I completed my B.S. at Tianjin University in 2016. I joined Vanderbilt Institute of Surgery Engineering as a graduate research assistant. I served as instructor in the department of computer science teaching courses CS 1104.
I'm working on data-driven machine learning, medical image analysis and translational research of medicine at Nvidia Research and Vanderbilt University

Before 2021
Thomas S. Huang 04/2020
I was greatly saddened to learn that Thomas S. Huang passed away, aged 84.
 
My own experience have no direct relation to professor Huang, but I have known many of his students in a large scale of areas. Professor Huang is known for his kindness, professional research in computer vision.
 
Professor Huang is a lighthouse in both academia and industry of computer vision, an idle for many young professionals. He was arguably an extreme point. He will very much be missed
